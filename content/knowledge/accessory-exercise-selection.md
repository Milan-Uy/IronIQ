## Accessory Exercise Selection

Accessory exercises are the supporting cast of a strength or hypertrophy program. The main lifts (squat, deadlift, bench, press, weighted pull-ups) are the stars — they provide the primary training stimulus and the majority of the strength adaptation. Accessories serve three specific purposes: correcting weaknesses in the main lifts, adding targeted hypertrophy, and balancing muscle development to reduce injury risk.

### Purpose 1: Weakness Correction

Every strength lift has a sticking point — the hardest part of the movement. Identifying and targeting the specific muscles responsible for the sticking point allows direct correction.

**Common weakness-accessory pairings:**

| Weakness | Likely Weak Muscle | Accessory |
|---|---|---|
| Squat: hips rise faster than bar (good morning) | Quads | Leg press, front squat, pause squat |
| Squat: knee cave | Hip abductors | Clamshells, abductor machine, lateral band walks |
| Deadlift: cannot break the floor | Quads, hamstrings | Romanian deadlift, leg press, deficit deadlift |
| Deadlift: upper back rounds | Upper back, lats | Barbell rows, cable rows, face pulls |
| Bench: stuck at lockout | Triceps | Close-grip bench, tricep pushdowns, JM press |
| Bench: stuck at the bottom | Chest, front delt | Dumbbell press, pause bench, incline press |
| Press: cannot complete overhead | Triceps, lateral delt | Lateral raises, close-grip press, push press |

### Purpose 2: Targeted Hypertrophy

Main compound lifts build muscle, but they do not equally develop all muscles. A bench press primarily loads the pecs and triceps — the biceps and rear deltoids need direct work. Accessories fill these gaps:

- **Back and rear delts:** Rows (cable, dumbbell, barbell), face pulls, rear delt flies
- **Biceps:** Barbell curl, dumbbell curl, hammer curl
- **Triceps:** Tricep pushdowns, skull crushers, overhead extensions
- **Lateral deltoids:** Lateral raises (dumbbell or cable)
- **Hamstrings:** Romanian deadlift, leg curl, Nordic curl
- **Glutes:** Hip thrust, cable kickback, Bulgarian split squat
- **Calves:** Standing calf raise, seated calf raise
- **Abs/core:** Hanging leg raise, ab wheel, cable crunch, plank variations

### Purpose 3: Balance and Injury Prevention

A program heavy on horizontal pressing (bench) requires horizontal pulling to balance the shoulder joint — otherwise, the front deltoids and pecs become dominant and tight while the rear deltoids and external rotators become weak and lengthened. This imbalance is a primary cause of shoulder injuries in lifters.

**Balancing principles:**
- For every horizontal press, include horizontal pulling (rows)
- For every vertical pull (pull-up), include vertical pressing (overhead press)
- Include direct rear delt work (face pulls, rear delt flies) if bench pressing heavily
- Include direct hamstring and glute work if squatting is your primary lower body movement

A common prescription: 1:1 pressing-to-pulling ratio by volume (same number of sets of rows as sets of bench), or even a 1:1.5 ratio (more pulling than pressing) for lifters who have pre-existing shoulder issues.

### How Many Accessories?

Less than you think. The most common mistake with accessories is doing too many, which creates unnecessary fatigue that impairs recovery for the main lifts. A practical framework per training session:

- 2–4 accessory exercises
- 2–4 sets each
- Higher rep ranges (8–15) than main lifts

The accessories should not leave you so fatigued that the next session's main lift suffers.

### Exercise Selection Principles

- **Prefer free weights and cables over machines** for most accessories — they require stabilizing muscles and allow more natural movement patterns. Exceptions: leg press, lat pulldown, cable variations, and isolation machines are all legitimate.
- **Keep accessories consistent** across a training block — you cannot assess whether an accessory is working if it changes every week. Run an accessory for at least 4–6 weeks before swapping it.
- **Simpler is better:** A program with 15 different accessory exercises is harder to track, harder to progressively overload, and more likely to cause confusion than a program with 4 well-chosen accessories tracked and loaded consistently over time.
